# Critic Scoring Rubric

All drafts are scored on four dimensions. Each dimension is rated 0.0–1.0.

**Final Score** = (Imagery × 0.30) + (Flow × 0.25) + (Hook × 0.25) + (Structure × 0.20)

---

## Dimension 1: Imagery (30%)

**What it measures:** Are the images specific, sensory, and non-cliché?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **0.0–0.3** | Abstract language dominates. Emotions named, not shown. Multiple clichés ("heart of gold," "tears in the rain"). No sensory grounding. |
| **0.4–0.6** | Some concrete images but they're generic. At least one original image. Feelings mostly told rather than shown. |
| **0.7–0.8** | Most images are specific and sensory. Reader can see/smell/hear the scene. Rare cliché. At least one image that earns surprise. |
| **0.9–1.0** | Every line earns its image. No clichés. At least 2 images a first-time reader would remember. Physicality is precise. |

**Anchor lines:**
- 0.3: *"You broke my heart and now I'm lost"*
- 0.7: *"Your jacket's still on the passenger seat, windows fogged, heat still running"*
- 1.0: *"You left the coffee mug half-full like you were planning to come back"*

---

## Dimension 2: Flow (25%)

**What it measures:** Do the lines move naturally when spoken aloud? Are syllable counts consistent?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **0.0–0.3** | Lines feel forced when spoken. Syllable counts vary wildly between lines. Unnatural stress patterns. |
| **0.4–0.6** | Some lines flow, others stumble. Inconsistent syllable targets. A few awkward word choices for meter. |
| **0.7–0.8** | Most lines scan cleanly. Syllable counts follow target ranges (verse: 8–10, chorus: 6–9). Stress falls naturally. |
| **0.9–1.0** | Every line feels inevitable when spoken. Syllables are consistent without feeling forced. The rhythm reinforces the emotion. |

**Test:** Read the draft aloud at performance speed. Mark any line where you stumble or have to rush/stretch to fit the meter.

---

## Dimension 3: Hook Strength (25%)

**What it measures:** Is the hook (chorus title/peak line) memorable, singable, and emotionally resonant?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **0.0–0.3** | No clear hook. Chorus doesn't differentiate from verses. Title not present or not memorable. |
| **0.4–0.6** | There is a hook attempt. It's functional but not sticky. Would not stand alone out of context. |
| **0.7–0.8** | The hook is clear and singable. A stranger could repeat it after one listen. Carries emotional weight. |
| **0.9–1.0** | The hook is the song. Impossible to hear once and forget. Works without the surrounding lyrics. Means more every time. |

**Test:** Read only the chorus. Does it carry the full emotional thesis of the song on its own?

---

## Dimension 4: Structural Coherence (20%)

**What it measures:** Does the draft have a clear emotional arc? Was the 4C Framework applied?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **0.0–0.3** | No discernible structure. Lines could be reordered without impact. No arc from opening to close. |
| **0.4–0.6** | Some structure present. Verse introduces a scene but pre-chorus/chorus relationship is unclear. 4C partially applied. |
| **0.7–0.8** | Clear verse → pre-chorus → chorus progression. Emotional state shifts meaningfully. 4C applied (north star line identifiable). |
| **0.9–1.0** | Every section serves a distinct role in the arc. The final line resolves or deepens the opening question. 4C fully executed. |

**Test:** Label each line: Setup / Shift / Payoff / Resolution. If you can't, the structure needs work.

---

## Score Interpretation

| Range | Status |
|---|---|
| 0.0–0.39 | Below baseline — do not commit |
| 0.40–0.59 | Developing — revert unless strong upward trend |
| 0.60–0.74 | Viable — commit if above previous baseline |
| 0.75–0.89 | Strong — commit and note the winning mutation |
| 0.90–1.0 | Exceptional — mark as benchmark output |
