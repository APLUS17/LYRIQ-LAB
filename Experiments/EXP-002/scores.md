## Critic Notes

### Draft A
- **Imagery:** Named places (Sunoco on Route Eight, barbershop, Cherry Street, Tommy Garland's house) anchor every abstraction. The layaway metaphor earns genuine surprise. One cliché in the bridge ("you don't grow up in a town / you grow up beside it") is abstract but acceptable as a landing. **0.75**
- **Flow:** Whisper gate clearly improved over EXP-001 Draft A (0.55 → 0.70). Most verse lines are 9–10 syllables with natural spoken stress. One chorus failure: "I made it something harder than it had to be" (12 syllables, stumbles mid-line). Two Verse 2 closer lines are muddled. The gate works — this draft is substantially more metered than EXP-001 A. **0.70**
- **Hook:** The north star couplet ("I kept them all on layaway for later / and later finally ran out on me") is the strongest hook candidate but it sits in Verse 1, not the chorus. Chorus hook ("I'm leaving in the morning / with all the things I almost said") is functional but not yet sticky. **0.65**
- **Structure:** Clear arc present (avoidance → arithmetic → thesis → specific face → philosophical). 4C not applied. Verse 2's closing couplet muddies the arc with meta-commentary ("I told myself a lot of things"). **0.68**

### Draft B
- **Imagery:** 4C pass preserved all specific imagery and upgraded one weak line ("like feeling it would make it mean the same"). The Tommy Garland stanza trimmed to its four strongest lines — the truck-in-driveway image and the one-second-two pause are now the sharpest images in the song. **0.80**
- **Flow:** 4C corrected the 12-syllable chorus bust. Chorus now reads cleanly at 7/8/10/7/9/9/10/9 — well within targets. Pre-chorus condensed to a single punchy 7-syllable line. Bridge left as a 2-line couplet; the restraint is rhythmically tight. Best flow in the experiment. **0.80**
- **Hook:** North star couplet elevated to chorus. "I kept them all on layaway for later / and later finally ran out" now lands as the emotional thesis. Verse 2 Tommy Garland image provides the specific human face of the thesis. Hook is singable and carries emotional weight on its own. **0.78**
- **Structure:** 4C fully executed. North star identified (layaway couplet), cuts removed competing content, carving sharpened three lines, connect arc is cleanly readable: setup (V1) → arithmetic (pre-ch) → thesis (chorus) → face (V2) → landing (bridge). **0.80**

### Draft C
- **Imagery:** Second-person inversion produces some of the sharpest images in the entire experiment: "I saw the headlights on the wall," "I knew the way you idle at that corner," "your jacket's been in the hall closet / the one you left the winter of oh-nine," "I pressed the little heart and set it down." The year-specific detail (oh-nine) earns real surprise. Bridge slightly over-explains ("some older version of the two of us"). **0.80**
- **Flow:** Verse 1 and Verse 2 stanza 1 are clean (8–10 syllables, natural stress). Two lines in Verse 2 stanza 2 break: "I keep it there in case you pass through needing it" (13 syllables) and "I know you won't, but I keep it there just fine" (12 syllables). Whisper gate was applied but information density overrode meter in the jacket stanza — the emotional weight of the image competed with rhythm. Chorus anaphora ("still around" repeated) scans well and is metered for effect. **0.73**
- **Hook:** "Still Around" anaphora chorus is the strongest hook of the three drafts. The structural repetition ("still around / still in the same house, same number / still awake at one a.m.") is immediately singable. "I don't know why I still expect you / but I do, I do" lands as a clean emotional close. A stranger could repeat this chorus. **0.82**
- **Structure:** Title-first approach produces cleaner natural structure than stream-of-consciousness. V1 (scene) → Pre-ch (pattern named) → Chorus (hook/thesis) → V2 (specific evidence) → Bridge (stakes). Arc holds. Bridge over-explains slightly. **0.76**

## Hypothesis Verdict

**CONFIRMED.** Flow improved across all three drafts compared to EXP-001 equivalents:

| Draft | EXP-001 Flow | EXP-002 Flow | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (raw) | 0.55 | 0.70 | **+0.15** |
| B (4C pass) | 0.70 | 0.80 | **+0.10** |
| C (alt angle) | 0.74 | 0.73 | -0.01 |
| **Average** | **0.663** | **0.743** | **+0.08** |

The whisper gate made the most impact on raw (Draft A) generation, where the lack of rhythm awareness was most costly. The 4C pass benefited from starting with cleaner input. Draft C's slight Flow regression is explained by the information-dense jacket stanza — the emotional payoff competed with rhythm and won.

**Secondary finding:** Draft C's Hook score (0.82) is the highest hook score in either experiment. The title-first / second-person approach may be worth isolating as a future hypothesis.

**Next hypothesis candidate:** "Title-first generation (as used in Draft C) will improve Hook scores across all three drafts because the hook is the gravity well the other lines orbit."
