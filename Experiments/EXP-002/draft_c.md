# EXP-002 / Draft C — Alternative Direction

**SOP:** 02_The_Spark_Engine (v0.1.1 — whisper-test gate active)
**Method:** Title First → "Still Around"
**Angle:** The person left behind — someone from the narrator's past who stayed in the hometown. Same night, different POV: they watched you leave once, now you're leaving again without stopping.
**Structural test:** Does a second-person address ("you") produce stronger hook than first-person drift?

---

## Verse 1

You drove past the house around midnight  
I saw the headlights on the wall  
I knew the way you idle at that corner  
ten-second pause, then nothing at all  

I almost went out to the porch  
stood at the door with my hand on the frame  
figured you knew where the lights were  
figured you'd come if you came  

## Pre-Chorus

been doing this since you were twenty-two  
I been still around, you been passing through  

## Chorus

still around  
still in the same house, same number  
still awake at one a.m.  
still around  
you can knock on this door anytime  
I'll still answer  
I don't know why I still expect you  
but I do, I do  

## Verse 2

Heard you loaded up last Tuesday  
Denise from next door texted me the news  
I pressed the little heart and set it down  
and went back to whatever I was doing  

your jacket's been in the hall closet  
the one you left the winter of oh-nine  
I keep it there in case you pass through needing it  
I know you won't, but I keep it there just fine  

## Bridge

I don't know what I'm still holding space for  
some older version of the two of us  
the one where you came back and it was easy  
the one where staying wasn't such a fuss
