**Angle:** The version of yourself you left there — all the things you kept meaning to do, still waiting

## Verse 1

Drove past the Sunoco on Route Eight tonight  
the one that used to be a corner diner  
I kept the window down till the cold got mean  
like if I felt it, that would make it final  

I meant to stop at every place I didn't  
the barbershop, the park on Cherry Street  
I kept them all on layaway for later  
and later finally ran out on me  

## Pre-Chorus

I been half-gone for years now  
I just didn't do the math  

## Chorus

I'm leaving in the morning  
with all the things I almost said  
the visits I kept rescheduling  
the calls I owed, the debts I fed  

this town don't hold it against me  
it just stayed the same and waited  
I made it something harder than it had to be  
and drove through it like I was running late  

## Verse 2

Pulled up in front of Tommy Garland's house  
the lights were on, his truck was in the driveway  
I thought about the knock — one second, two  
then put the car in drive and pulled away  

I told myself he knew what he meant to me  
I told myself the gesture wasn't needed  
I told myself a lot of things that year  
and most of them I mostly didn't mean it  

## Bridge

You don't grow up in a town  
you grow up beside it  
looking out the window  
while it happens without you
