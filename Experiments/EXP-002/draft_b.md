## 4C Notes

**COPY (north star identification):**
Strongest line in Draft A: *"I kept them all on layaway for later / and later finally ran out on me"*
— Specific, surprising, speaks to the whole song's thesis in two lines.
Secondary anchor: *"I meant to stop at every place I didn't"* — earns its simplicity.

**CUT:**
- Cut Verse 2 entirely: the Tommy Garland stanza over-explains the Verse 1 thesis. The specificity of one name is good, but the closing couplet ("I told myself a lot of things that year / and most of them I mostly didn't mean it") is muddled and redundant.
- Cut Bridge stanzas 3–4: "looking out the window / while it happens without you" softens what should land hard — keep only the first two lines.
- Cut Chorus lines 5–8: "this town don't hold it against me / it just stayed the same and waited" is stronger than "I made it something harder than it had to be / and drove through it like I was running late" — keep the former, cut the latter pair.
- Cut Pre-Chorus line 2: "I just didn't do the math" is clever but deflates the emotion. Let "I been half-gone for years now" stand alone.

**CARVE:**
- "Drove past the Sunoco on Route Eight tonight" → keep exactly; grounded, specific, metered.
- "like if I felt it, that would make it final" → upgrade: "like feeling it would make it mean the same" — the specificity of *feeling vs. knowing* sharpens the emotional beat.
- "I meant to stop at every place I didn't" → this is the north star's setup; keep verbatim.
- Chorus restructure: move the north star couplet ("layaway") to chorus position; let it anchor the emotional peak.
- "this town don't hold it against me / it just stayed the same and waited" → carve to: "this town just held the same position / same as I left it, same as always" — removes the personification ("held it against me"), which borders on cliché.

**CONNECT:**
Arc reads: *avoidance (V1) → arithmetic of absence (pre-ch) → thesis of the unsaid (chorus) → specific face of the unsaid (V2 fragment) → landing (bridge)*
- Add one Verse 2 image from Tommy Garland stanza: keep "Pulled up in front of Tommy Garland's house / the lights were on, his truck was in the driveway" and "I thought about the knock — one second, two / then put the car in drive and pulled away." Cut the rest.
- This creates: Verse 1 (general drift) → Verse 2 (one specific face) → Bridge (philosophical landing).

## Verse 1

Drove past the Sunoco on Route Eight tonight  
the one that used to be a corner diner  
I kept the window down till the cold got mean  
like feeling it would make it mean the same  

I meant to stop at every place I didn't  
the barbershop, the park on Cherry Street  
I kept them all on layaway for later  
and later finally ran out on me  

## Pre-Chorus

I been half-gone for years now  

## Chorus

I'm leaving in the morning  
with all the things I almost said  
I kept them all on layaway for later  
and later finally ran out  

this town just held the same position  
same as I left it, same as always  
I drove through it at fifty in the dark  
like I was somewhere I was going  

## Verse 2

Pulled up in front of Tommy Garland's house  
the lights were on, his truck was in the driveway  
I thought about the knock — one second, two  
then put the car in drive and pulled away  

## Bridge

You don't grow up in a town  
you grow up beside it
