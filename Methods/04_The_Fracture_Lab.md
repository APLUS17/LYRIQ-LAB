## Core Technique

> *To be populated from source PDF.*

**Baseline logic (until PDF is parsed):**

The Fracture Lab operates on the premise that the best lyric lives one step past "too weird to use." It takes a working phrase and deliberately breaks it through substitution, inversion, removal, or collision.

Core operations:
- **Substitution**: Replace the expected word with its opposite, its cause, or its cousin
- **Inversion**: Flip the syntax or the point of view
- **Removal**: Delete the verb. What's left? Delete the subject. What's left?
- **Collision**: Force two unrelated images into the same line

## Prompt Template

> *To be populated from source PDF.*

**Baseline prompt:**
```
FRACTURE LAB — Phrase Mutation Prompt

Input phrase: [your current lyric line]

Apply each operation and write the result:
1. SUBSTITUTE: Replace [key word] with its emotional opposite.
2. INVERT: Reverse the cause and the effect in the line.
3. REMOVE: Cut the verb. Cut the subject. What fragment survives?
4. COLLIDE: Merge this line with an image from a completely different context.

Keep the result that surprises you the most. Discard the others.
```

## Scoring Anchor

Fracture Lab improvements show up in **Imagery (30%)** scores.
The Critic looks for: at least one phrase per draft that couldn't have been predicted from the setup.
A reverted mutation usually means the fracture was arbitrary rather than earned.
