# 03 — The Feeling Machine

**Status:** ACTIVE
**Source:** SongwriterCore 100 Songstarter Prompts (Flashback Feels, Fear & Pressure, Love & Longing, Identity, Undefined Connections) · Creative Foundations Vol.2
**Note:** SongwriterCore_Write_Songs_That_Work.pdf may contain additional technique. Upload when available.
**Purpose:** Framework for emotional specificity — moving from vague sentiment to precisely felt experience. The Lyricist uses this before generating to lock in the emotional frequency.

---

## Key Concepts

Most lyrics name emotions. The Feeling Machine trains you to *inhabit* the precise texture of an emotion instead.

The distinction:
- **Weak**: "I'm lonely" → names the emotion
- **Strong**: "The relief of finally crying in a parking lot at 2am" → textures the emotion

SongwriterCore's prompt categories are emotional frequency maps. Each one anchors you to a different corner of human experience before you write a single lyric.

From Creative Foundations:
> "Idea Curation: Before you create, you collect and curate. Organize inspiration, build Reference Stacks, tag samples, study what moves you. Fuel matters."

The Feeling Machine IS the curation step applied to emotional raw material.

---

## Core Technique

**Step 1 — Choose your frequency.** Pick one SongwriterCore category that matches the emotional territory of the song.

**Step 2 — Name the subtype.** Don't say "sad." Say "the sadness of realising someone gave up on you before telling you." Use the prompts as a chisel.

**Step 3 — Find the object.** Every emotion has a physical carrier — a half-read text, a jacket left on a chair, a voicemail saved for no reason. Find it.

**Step 4 — Write from the object.** Describe the object so that someone who has never felt this emotion can feel it by reading your description.

---

## Emotional Frequency Map (SongwriterCore)

**For nostalgia / unresolved past:**
> "Write about a part of your past that feels unresolved."
> "Name something you miss even though you know it was not good for you."
> "Write about something from your past that still affects how you love."

**For anxiety / avoidance:**
> "Write about the area of your life that feels the most fragile right now."
> "Write about what scares you about actually getting what you want."
> "Name a fear you often justify with logic."

**For love and disconnection:**
> "Write about what missing someone actually feels like to you."
> "Write about what makes you emotionally pull away."
> "Write about an ending that never really happened."

**For identity tension:**
> "Write about the part of yourself you edit around others."
> "Write about something you pretend does not matter but does."
> "Write about something you are actively unlearning."

**For in-between / undefined:**
> "What does it feel like when nothing is wrong but nothing is right?"
> "Have you ever pretended not to care when you did?"
> "What does uncertainty do to your behaviour?"

## Prompt Template

```
FEELING MACHINE — Pre-Generation Lock

Step 1 — Pick the frequency:
  Category: [choose from the map above]
  Prompt: [specific prompt from that category]

Step 2 — Name the texture:
  Not "[emotion]" but "[specific subtype of emotion]"
  e.g. not "longing" but "the longing for a version of someone who no longer exists"

Step 3 — Find the object:
  What physical thing carries this emotion in the song?
  (e.g. a half-typed message, a voicemail you saved, a coat that still has their smell)

Step 4 — Write:
  Describe the object. One paragraph. No lyrics yet.
  A stranger should be able to feel the emotion without being told its name.

Step 5 — Enter the Spark Engine with that object as your anchor.
```

---

## Scoring Anchor

Strong Feeling Machine work shows up in **Imagery (30%)** scores above 0.70.
The Critic's test: "Could a reader who has never felt this emotion feel it from the imagery alone?"
If the answer is no, the Feeling Machine step was skipped or shallow.
