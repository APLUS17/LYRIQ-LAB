## Core Technique

> *To be populated from source PDF.*

**Baseline logic (until PDF is parsed):**

Most lyrics name emotions ("I'm lonely," "I'm in love"). The Feeling Machine trains you to *show* the precise texture of the emotion instead.

The technique:
1. Name the emotion's *subtype* — not "happy" but "the relief of someone finally understanding you"
2. Find the physical sensation that carries it — where do you feel it in your body?
3. Find the mundane object or action that has become emotionally charged
4. Write from the object, not the feeling

## Prompt Template

> *To be populated from source PDF.*

**Baseline prompt:**
```
FEELING MACHINE — Precision Prompt

Step 1 — Identify: What is the emotion's exact texture?
  (e.g., "the embarrassment of realizing you overshared" not "embarrassment")

Step 2 — Locate: Where does this feeling live in the body?
  (e.g., "a tightness behind the sternum, not painful, just present")

Step 3 — Find the object: What ordinary thing carries this emotion?
  (e.g., a half-read text, a parking lot at 2am, a hoodie left on a chair)

Step 4 — Write: Describe the object as if someone who has never felt this emotion
  could feel it just by reading your description.
```

## Scoring Anchor

High Feeling Machine scores improve **Imagery (30%)** and **Hook strength (25%)**.
The Critic asks: "Does a stranger reading this feel the emotion without being told its name?"
Benchmark: 0.7+ Imagery score indicates the Feeling Machine is working.
