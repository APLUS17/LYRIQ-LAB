## Core Technique

**Key ratios to track:**

| Ratio | What it measures | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Line yield** | Lines generated → lines kept post-4C | 10:1 (keep 1 per 10 generated) |
| **Draft yield** | Drafts written → drafts worth developing | 3:1 (1 keeper per 3 drafts) |
| **Session yield** | Sessions run → sessions producing a new high score | Improving trend over time |
| **Mutation success rate** | Genome mutations kept → mutations reverted | >50% kept = Brain is improving |

**The compound effect:**
Creativity compounds. The more often you show up and engage with your process, the easier it becomes to find flow and finish. Small actions lead to big momentum.

## Yield Log Format

Append to `results.tsv` after every experiment. The yield numbers go in the Notes column.

```
Session yield log format:
- Lines generated: ___
- Lines surviving post-4C: ___
- Lines scoring ≥0.70 on Imagery: ___
- Draft score (avg 3 drafts): ___
- Delta vs. baseline: +/- ___
- Yield ratio: [kept/generated]
- Ignition workout: yes/no
```

## Scoring Anchor

Creative Yield Math is a meta-SOP — it observes the health of all other SOPs.
The Researcher uses yield data to identify which prompt categories and which Genome mutations consistently produce high-yield sessions.

**Flags to watch:**
- SOP yield ratio below 0.05 over 5 consecutive experiments → flag that SOP for mutation
- Session yield ratio rising over time → the Genome is improving
- Session yield ratio flat or falling despite new mutations → the mutations are solving the wrong problem

This is the compound interest of the system. Every logged session makes the next hypothesis smarter.
