# 06 — The 4C Framework

**Status:** ACTIVE
**Source:** Creative Foundations Vol.2 — Sound + Creativity (Hermes) · Ch.4: 15 Quick Wins
**Purpose:** Four-step editing and refinement system applied by the Architect to all raw Lyricist output. This is the core transformation pass.

---

## Key Concepts

From Creative Foundations — directly quoted:
> "**The 4C Framework**: Copy your loop across your project for 4 minutes, then Cut, Carve, and Connect the sections."

And from the Idea Development quick win:
> "**Ruthlessly Cut**: Ask yourself, 'Does this sound have a purpose?' If not, cut it."

From Idea Review:
> "**One Sit Listen**: No tweaking allowed. Just listen. Then take honest notes with timestamps."
> "**3 Questions**: What do I love? What feels off? What's missing?"

The 4C Framework is the structured version of these instincts, applied sequentially. You cannot skip steps — each pass depends on the previous one.

---

## Core Technique

```
COPY   → Find the strongest existing line. This is your north star.
CUT    → Remove everything that competes with or dilutes the north star line.
CARVE  → Sharpen what remains: tighter syllables, harder words, cleaner images.
CONNECT → Ensure surviving lines form a single emotional arc from first to last.
```

**COPY in detail:**
Read the draft once without stopping. Circle the one line you would never cut. That line IS the song. Everything else must earn its place next to it. If you can't identify it, the draft needs more Spark Engine work first.

**CUT in detail:**
Go line by line. Ask: "Does this line make the north star line stronger, or does it compete with it?" Cut at least 30% of the draft. If cutting feels hard, you haven't found the right north star. The Creative Foundations test: "Does this sound/line have a purpose? If not, cut it."

**CARVE in detail:**
For each surviving line:
- Replace soft words: nice, beautiful, very, feel, think, just, really → concrete alternatives
- Recount syllables. Verse target: 8–10. Pre-chorus: 6–8. Chorus: 6–9.
- Eliminate filler: just, really, kind of, so, sort of, a little, kind of, sort of

**CONNECT in detail:**
Read the draft aloud at performance pace. Map the arc:
- Line 1: What state is the listener put in?
- Middle: Where does the shift happen?
- Final line: What are they left with — resolution, open wound, or question?
If the arc isn't clear after reading aloud, reorder or bridge.

Creative Foundations: "Arrangement Borrow: Use a proven song structure from a song you love as a guide. Customize it from there."

---

## The 3 Review Questions (from Creative Foundations)

Apply these during the CONNECT pass:
1. **What do I love?** — This is your COPY. Protect it.
2. **What feels off?** — This is your CUT list.
3. **What's missing?** — This is what the CARVE + CONNECT pass adds.

## Scoring Anchor

The 4C Framework is the primary driver of **Structural coherence (20%)** and has measurable impact on all four dimensions.
The Critic compares draft_a (raw Spark Engine) vs draft_b (post-4C) — a well-applied 4C pass typically improves total score by 0.10–0.20.
A flat delta means the north star line was chosen incorrectly or CUT was too conservative.
